Editorial Design Editorial Design is the ultimate in a user experience journey. The homepage starts with a cover that must get a user to pick up the magazine and buy it and read it. It needs to draw a user in with strong visuals.

Visuals vary greatly between magazine types— for a teen magazine a teenage girl wants to see someone like herself and some cute boys. An engineer needs something more complex and is not as straight forward. How do you explain the complex transfer of energy between countries? With a map? Some graphs? A family looking for advice on buying a new car might want to see something about cars that isn't what they would see in a car dealership.

About

I have a design degree from School of Visual Arts in New York City, and have completed Le Wagon's Full Stack 9 week bootcamp.

These two educations give me the unique characteristic of being both a designer and coder.

I make websites and SaaS products– combining code and design for quick beautiful solutions.

I often design with code– directly in the browser saving time and money. When designing in code, we become aware of dificulties that may arise from a design, and can tackle them early in the process.

Note to Companies Looking for help
I can create quick iterations from wireframes and sketches to beautifully coded websites and interfaces.

Note to developers creating products
With the wireframes created, I can code them into pixel perfect interfaces. I have done full stack work with Rails, Django and React, and understand how to communicate with the backend people.

Special note to CEO’s, COO's…
I specialize in startups needing quick deployment. As a startup, you want the most for your money. Hire me and I will do your frontend design, UI and code simultaneously.
