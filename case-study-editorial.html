Business Case

Listed above is a sampling of some of the newspapers and magazines I have worked for. The target user groups is often different from one publication to the next, but they all share a goal to give a reader access to information that is specifically tailored for their interests.

The Challenge

In order for a publication to give a profit, it needs to be able to get users/readers to want to read it, and become involved with the content for a period of time. It is best if they want to read more than one issue. It has to look like itself. Things such as Colors, fonts, layout styles need to be in the same style in order to create unity. It needs to be visually interesting for its target group. It also has a very firm deadline. Printers to not wait.

Project Scope & Objectives

Magazines have a lot in common even when they have very different readers. They all need:

» Design and content that engages users.

» Covers that make you want to open the magazine.

» To create a desire for readers to pick up and purchase the magazine.

» To be easy to navigate.

» To keep a user engaged.

» To be sent to the printer ontime.

By achieving these objectives the reader should want to come back again when the next issue becomes available.

The Process of creating a magazine or newspaper

Step 1 Meet with editors and writers, learn about the stories coming up, and start the process of what visual will be used. Things considered: Photography? (Stock photo, or a hired photograph) Illustration (self made or needs to hire an illustrator) ? Type only treatment? — in financial stories, are the numbers ready to be made into a graphic? Are there extra numbers that can be used to highlight the story?

Step 2 After initial meetings, start looking for what to use as a visualization.

Step 3 Commission art with photographer, illustrator.

Step 4 Start to layout the story (in InDesign).

Step 5 Show layout to managing editor.

Step 6 Make changes as needed.

Step 7 Sign off on the design.

Step 8 Send to editors to edit the story to fit.

Step 9 Make color tests of the photographs.

Step 10 Cover design. Decide on image. Get headlines. Place them in the file. See if they fit in a visually pleasing way. Make edits. This is an iterative process.

Step 11 Send to the printer.

In addition to the above process style sheets and templates created to keep a cohesive look, and make it easier to make layouts.
