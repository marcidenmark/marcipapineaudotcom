The Challenge
Lyndgaard Media
Media, Public Relations, and Communications Company based in Copenhagen

Lyndgaard Media is a one woman company specialising in journalism, public relations and communications. The owner, Stine Lyndgaard, was starting out on her own after years of a successful career in tv and print journalism. She had an outdated 2 screen business card like website. She needed an entire redesign and rethinking of how to tell the story to keep and add to her client base.

The website needed to showcase a successful career to both get new clients, as well as keep existing ones.

Design wise, Stine had a really nice logo in the font Didot which would be used, so I needed to choose fonts that would pair well with it. The fonts to be used, needed to be free.

It was imperative to work on mobile.

Solution

With previous work as a magazine designer and art director I learned the art of telling a story. I used this experience to guide Stine in defining her company.

Process

I started with a wireframe showing how it could look, and what information it could have. This wireframe was used to write the text for the site.

Upon acceptance of the wireframe I started the design directly with code which made it easy to see how the site was working in real time. I was able to try different fonts globaly on the site by changing 2 lines of code.

I found a stock photo for inspiration and used this to art direct the photographer. (note to self: I think ths can be a caption)

Rational for design solutions

I created it as a one page site in order to minimise the need for clicks. We used a screen grab from a television newscast and logos of clients to add immediate credibility.

Typography decisions: The font Georgia is able to be usde for free, and it worked well with the pre-existing logo. It was paired with a simple sans serif, Open Sans.

Colors Were kept subdued and classic.
