Børsen
Business and Financial newspaper headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark
Business Case

Leading Danish business/financial newspaper in Denmark– read by industry leaders, and used to make actionable decisions. Their need was to improve the usability of their information graphics.

The Challenge

What was needed was to have information graphics that readers could use to help in decision making. Børsen's readers are busy. They don't have a lot of time to read the paper, yet it is an important part of their day. Information needs to be understood quickly. Børsen has A LOT of information graphics, as they often help tell a story of a thousand words in 5-10 seconds.

The graphics at the time were difficult to understand. They were represented in units that don't match. Units of measure were hard to find. The story was confused.

Marci has sent Børsen on a most needed journey to get infographics in the newspaper that the readers can actually read and understand.
Jørgen Andresen Head of Media at Dagbladet Børsen
Project Scope & Objectives

A review of the process of how graphics were being created from start to end was to be made so that there could be new guidelines on how to create a usable graphic that was easy to understand.

The ultimate goal was to find a way to make sure the graphic was written and designed with information that made sense and was easy to understand.

Finding the Story and Teaching How to Tell it

The start was a review of the existing graphics to see what did and didn't work. Below is the start of that story.

Things to improve in a simple graphic
Graphic on the left is the older graphic. Graphic on the right is the redesigned one using the following principles.
1. Adding a subhead to the headline to pull readers into graphic. 2. Placing the unit of measure on the top of the numbers instead of the headline of the graphic. 3. Getting rid of the many decimal points for clarity. 4. Got rid of the date of the month as the month was enough. 5. Added horizontal lines to make the graph easier to read.
Quick value to a story in a new type of graphic.
Old graphic on the left. New graphic on the right.
The older graphic had was eye catching but the story wasn't explained very well. It said there had been some ups and downs– ending high. But the fact that the the end of the graphic is an estimate can't easily be seen. There was also no easy way to understand the time frame of the graphic.

The graphic on the left is both quick and easy to understand. Already adding the years on the bottom (the sketched in small tics) starts to tell more. There is a key on the bottom tells the quantity of one circle. and you get the answer to the story in a few seconds. If you are excited by the graphic you would read dive into the story.

The Solution

Guidelines and processes were setup from the start of a writer requesting a graphic all the way until it would be proofread before publication.

✓ Improved communication and working processes between art and editorial departments

✓ Reworked paper’s information graphics and created style guide.

✓ Laid out new rules and guidelines on how to:

✓ Create a good information graphic.

✓ Choose the correct graphic form to communicate the core message.

✓ Present graphics that are quick and easy to understand.

✓ Use typography and line styles and weights to communicate multiple levels of information.
