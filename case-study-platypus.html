Platypus
Sartup working with Human Resource software
Business Case

Platypus is a Copenhagen based startup working within HR. Its focus is to help companies make good hires, and retain talent.

The Challenge

Platypus was in an extremely early stage of development. They had a low tech prototype that was successfully used to secure a round of pre-seed funding and early stage users.

This next iteration should greatly enhance the experience for early stage users with an easy to use interface. And continue to get new companies to buy into the product.

There are 2 core parts of the product- an in depth user profile completion process, and a dashboard for onboarded companies to utilize the information collected from new onboards.

Project Scope & Objectives

With a 3 month deadline the product needed a range of functions such as:

» An user creation interface that would work on mobile and desktop

» A multi screen dashboard displaying the data gathered during the user onboarding process

» A more robust working product with solid UX/UI

» Improved usability of product

» Less clicks and more condensed information presented on on fewer screens

» An inhanced way to visualize the data– the core aspect of the product

» A replacement to sending information to users via a PDF attached to an email

» Keep existing clients happy and excited for upcoming features

» Continue to help selling product to new companies

» Bring in more funding

The Outcome

✓ A new round of funding of over 1 million kroner

✓ A signup flow including multiple levels of information that worked on mobile and desktop

✓ A product where dropdowns were eliminated, and buttons were created in stead

✓ CSS broken into components for easy reuse

✓ CSS Grid coded for easy completion of all future layouts

✓ Entire new way to filter that eliminated the need for complicated dropdown

✓ Coded HTML emails used during the signup process (instead of PDFs) Based on investor's feedback about it being difficult to get people to read and complete their emails.

My Role

There was a team of 5. We had weekly product meetings, where we reviewed what we were working on. This was the spring board to define the weeks goals. And decide on what features to work on.

The way the product would be designed and used was still under construction. The founders had a strong idea of what the product should do, but how it would do that was under review constantly. Some ideas were taken into production, while others were not.

I contributed to showing how we would make their ideas come to life. As a coder, I was always aware of how difficult or easy a feature was to create.

Some of the great ideas that were discovered needed to be put on hold because of time demands it would have.

The Process

Weekly Product Meetings Once a week we met with the entire team to review what we were woring on, and voice opinions, and review what we were working on. We spoke about features in progress, and coming up.

Dashboard research Tons of reviews of exhisting dashboard layouts for inspiration. Together with the UXer, we looked at many different dashboards. Some were for products that were similar to ours, and others that were not, but had good ways to visulaize information.

UI Research I constantly looked for inspiration for great UI design. One of the places I often go to is Steve Schoger's book, "Refactoring UI." [ If you're curious, you can see a few of his tips here ]

Grapic Research There was a JS library already being used. I looked into different options for visualizing what we needed.

Coding Research Looked into the different ways the layout could be coded. One of the things I know from the start was that I wanted to code a grid that could easily be used throughout the product. One of the considerations was Flexbox? Grid? Grid won.

Coding Built pages writting HTML & CSS– working closely with the backend developer and UXer.

Data Visualization Research Looked into how to show the data so it could be easily understodd. Part of the considerations involved different libraries to use.

Coding Emails I had not done this before. But found it really important to get away from sending information via pdfs in an email. Emails can be tricky because they need to be written in an extremely old type of html (using tables). Plux, there is a lot of standard CSS that doesn't work.

Typography & Colors Were supplied from the UXer.

Rational for Design Solutions

New way to show information instead of dropdown menus Upon starting the project there were many dropdown menus that were hard to navigate. I knew it would be good with an option, and during research came across "Improving the usability of multi-selecting from a long list" on Medium. It even had data showing longer retention by using other options. [ Read Medium Article ]

Desktop First Dashboard Wait, what did I just say?! I saw Søren Engelbrecht, Senior Mobile Strategist at A.P. Moller - Maersk give a talk, "Mobile When Necessary" This shattered my idea of mobile first ALWAYS. Søren's story spoke of Mærsk's large shipping company and their digital needs. They of course have work needs to be caried out while working on a multiple large screen because there are multiple levels of complicated information. They will never be able to do this on their cell phones on the way home from work. So NO to mobile design. But, other things are done almost entirely on a small device while workers are out on the docks. For example checking invetories upon arrival. It makes perfect sence of course.

So, With this in mind, and research with the Platypus founders who have years of experience working in HR: said they didn't use their phones for complicated HR work, as there was too much you needed to see at once, in order for it to make sence.

Mobile First Signup and User Onboarding Interface On the other hand, when users are signing up for the product, they are often NOT at a computer screen. So phones were the highest priority. The design and code was written for a mobile screen first, and then transfered to desktop, which worked rather seamlessly as how it would work there was considered while woring on the mobile screens.

A visual way to click on selections insteead of multiple dropdowns.
